# Change Management Procedure
Shows how to request, approve, test, and implement changes safely.

## Purpose
To provide a step-by-step process for requesting, reviewing, approving, implementing, and documenting changes.

## Steps

### 1. Submit Change Request
- Change Owner completes a Change Request Form.
- Include purpose, scope, risk assessment, testing plan, and rollback plan.

### 2. Classify the Change
- **Standard:** Pre-approved, low-risk, routine.
- **Normal:** Requires CAB review and approval.
- **Emergency:** Needed to fix critical issues; expedited approval.

### 3. Impact Assessment
- IT and Security teams assess operational, security, and business impact.
- Identify affected systems, users, and dependencies.

### 4. Approval
- Standard changes: auto-approved.
- Normal changes: CAB approval required.
- Emergency changes: management approval required.

### 5. Implementation
- Implement change according to the approved plan.
- Follow testing and validation steps.
- Document any deviations.

### 6. Post-Implementation Review
- Confirm change success.
- Review logs, errors, and user feedback.
- Emergency changes must undergo full review.

### 7. Documentation & Closure
- Update change records.
- Attach evidence (screenshots, logs, test results).
- Close the change ticket.

## Responsibilities
- Change Owner: Documentation and execution.
- IT/Security: Review and validation.
- CAB: Approval of Normal changes.
